Our Mission
The Maine Jr. Mariners mission is to build a competitive and winning 18U through Junior level Hockey teams, while offering an exciting brand of hockey. We will strive to capture the support of the Biddeford/York County community and make them proud to call the Jr. Mariners their team.
Through optimized player development, our coaching staff will strive to produce college and professional caliber players and well-rounded individuals who will represent the community with respect and integrity Our team is committed to making a positive impact in Biddeford by presenting exciting, family entertainment, and helping our sponsors and partners grow their brands and businesses.
The Jr. Mariners are committed to making a difference in Biddeford where we will live, play, and call home. Our players and coaches will be active members of the community and will be involved in numerous programs and outreach events where we can serve and enrich the lives of others. Jr. Mariners players will be available for big and small events and will bring fun-loving, enthusiastic energy with them wherever they go.

Academics
The high school years are a pivotal point in a student-athlete's life. The Maine Jr Mariners Academy is an innovation way to incorporate the pursuit of hockey development along with achieving academic success. Weather in pursuit of future NCAA opportunities in the classroom, or on the ice, it is imperative that the emphasis is applied to individual development during these formidable years.
Our Academy's model is to provide an environment that eliminates the time, logistics, and frustration association with balancing hockey training, and educational advancement. Our goal is to encourage, to support and partner with our students in order to develop their mental, physical, athletic and scholastic development.
As a student athlete at Maine Jr. Mariner Academy, our students reside in St. Joseph's College dorm facilities, attend VLACS Virtual Learning Academy Charter Schools or take level 100 classes at St. Josephs Collage to keep you NCAA eligible, and train and practice up to five times a week. You also have the opportunity to continue with any schooling you may be currently enrolled in. By providing all of these vital components within one program plus having current NCAA Hockey coaches on staff eliminates the hurdles association with NCAA eligibility, long drives times to practice, late study hours, homeschooling options, or living without familiar billets.
Through our unique leadership development, life skills are cerebral training, our students-athletes will be prepared for Life's future challenges after graduation graduation and to succeed at the next level.

Teams
18U AAA(National Bound)
The Maine Jr. Mariners 18U AAA(National Bound) team will play in the NAPHL (approximately 28 regular season games plus minimum of 4 playoff games), TIER 1 Hockey Federation (which will play 3-4 showcases for a minimum of 12-16 games) and Beast Series (10-16p games). All three leagues are premier midget leagues in North American and play the best competition. The 18U AAA(National Bound) team is owned and operated by the Maine Jr. Mariners LLC.. We will be working in tandem with multiple NAHL and EHL teams to offer players the chance for in-season promotions that very few teams can complete with.
18U AA
The Maine Jr. Mariners 18U AA team will play in the Beast Series (8-12 games) and E9 teams (8-12 games). The 18U AA team will From September through November.

Staff
Zachary Lebowitz (Head Hockey Ops, Head Coach/GM)
Zach Lebowitz, a current NCAA assistant coach at Bowdoin college with 7 years of experience at the NCAA level, and a former NCAA D3 goaltender and NEPSAC Champion has been coaching ice hockey and developing both skaters and goaltenders for over 10 years. After a career ending knee injury, he left Western New England University to achieve his BSBA in Finance from Suffolk University, and his MBA from Southern New Hampshire University. After completing degrees, he had a short career in finance working in Hedge Funds for State Street Bank. Zach has had several stops along the way in his hockey career including 6 years as an assistant at Tufts University, a brief stint as Head Coach at the prep level, and a current position with the Groton School (NEPSAC), Bowdoin College (NESCAC), Boston Jr Rangers (EHL(Assistant Coach) and EHLP (Head Coach). As a current Level 4 certified coach, Zach looks to bring an amazing attitude and great work ethic to his new EHL and EHLP organization. Zach, an Andover, MA native and current Amesbury, MA resident with his gorgeous wife and his 2 kids currently owns and operates Edge Hockey Skills, where he runs weekly clinics for skaters and goalies at the Pro, NCAA, Junior. Prep/High School, and youth hockey levels. Having coached at the high school, prep, Junior Tier II and Tier III Levels as well as the NCAA level he brings loads of experience to build a new expansion program into a successful business. Using his network, he believes he can help promote many of his players to higher junior levels and the collegiate levels. Zach has extensive experience alongside several great coaches like current director of goaltending for the NY Islanders Mitch Korn, Current NCAA D3 Head Coach Pat Norton and Matt Keating, current NCAA D3 Head Coach, former NCAA D1 Interim Head Coach and former ECHL Head Coach Ben Guite, former Boston Bruins Skating Coach and current NCAA D1 Assistant Coach Kim Brandvold. Learning from accomplished coaches and continuing to be a student of the game has provided Zach with a drive for coaching student-athletes both on and off the ice to become exceptional in hockey and life.
